--- url_pdf: https://arxiv.org/abs/2101.05774 abstract: We propose a procedure, which combines hierarchical clustering with a test of overidentifying restrictions for selecting valid instrumental variables (IV) from a large set of IVs. Some of these may be invalid in that they fail the exclusion restriction. We show that if the largest group of IVs is valid, our method achieves oracle properties. Unlike existing techniques, our method addresses weak instruments, multiple endogenous regressors and heterogeneous treatment effects. In simulations our procedure outperforms the Hard Thresholding and the Confidence Interval method.
